In this section, you can generate simple quality control curves for the port sampling data on species presence. Curves are produced separately for each of the different groups of organisms sampled in a given port (mobile epifauna, phytoplankton, fouling, benthos, zooplankton and pathogens).
The theoretical background of these curves is the “species–area curve” or the relevé method for defining the minimum area, or number, of samples needed to characterise the species community. In a simplified way when the curve goes flat the number of samples has been sufficient to find the species present in the sampled area. If the curve does not flatten but continues raising it is likely that more species would have been found with more samples were taken (observations).
