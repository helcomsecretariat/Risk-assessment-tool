Please note that before starting the process below contact should be made with the national administrations for both the origin and destination ports.
Step 1: Port Sampling and analysis using the agreed protocol
Perform port surveys in origin and destination ports, following the port survey protocol described in the JHP (Section 2, Annex 1) or get access to the results of such surveys done by others.

The agreed JHP methods are cost efficient and possible to do from the pier (no diving or boats required).

Port sampling results will be valid for a maximum of 5 years

From the date of the first of the two sampling visits (spring bloom). A Contracting Party may decide on a shorter validity for a port survey due to e.g. sensitiveness of the area, intensity of traffic or need for updated port survey data on non-indigenous species.
Step 2: Upload port sampling data to this tool
Applicants should submit the port survey data to the HELCOM or OSPAR Secretariats to be uploaded to this joint regional risk assessment tool (see Data tab).
Step 3: Run the risk assessment
Run the risk assessment for the ports (see Routes tab). If both ports are already in the system, it may be possible to get an indicative risk assessment for the route before starting with step 1.
Step 4: Apply for exemption
Applicants should attach the results of the risk assessment (see Routes tab) to an application fulfilling other national requirements and submit for the consideration of the port States. Official Contact Points for BWMC A-4 Exemptions. Please note that this list is updated annually.

More information can be found in the IMO Global Integrated Shipping Information System

Please note that the port States may also have other considerations in their final decision.
